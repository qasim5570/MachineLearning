Encode Other Categorical Columns:

2. High Cardinality - frequency encoding

Reason: 
  1. XGBoost doesn't recognize strings. 
  2. Also, one-hot encoding is not suitable here

Process:
  1. Categorical Columns - dtype objects for Pandas
  2. Replace Nan
  3. Number of unique columns/values inside each categorical column

Example Scenarios:
Example A: The "Rare Email" Signal (Frequency Encoding)
A fraudster creates a one-time email on an obscure domain like pookmail.com.

Before: It’s just a string.

After Frequency Encoding: It gets a value of 1.

Fraud Pattern: The model learns that transactions where Email_Freq == 1 are 90% more likely to be fraud than those where Email_Freq == 450,000 (Gmail).

Example B: The "Consistency" Signal (M-Flags)
The M columns check if the address on the card matches the shipping address.

Before: You have "T" (True), "F" (False), and "Unknown."

After Label Encoding: These become 1, 0, and 2.

Fraud Pattern: The model notices that when multiple M columns are 0 (False) simultaneously, it indicates a "Stolen Card" pattern where the thief doesn't know the victim's billing address.

Example C: The "Product Type" Signal (ProductCD)
Before: ProductCD is "W" (Service) or "C" (Commercial).

After Label Encoding: They become 4 and 0.

Fraud Pattern: Certain product codes have much higher chargeback rates. The model can now "weight" transactions for ProductCD 0 more heavily than others.
